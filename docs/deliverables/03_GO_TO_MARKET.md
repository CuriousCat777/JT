# Guardian One: Go-to-Market Strategy
## Beachhead Market, Positioning, Investor Pitch

---

## 1. POSITIONING STATEMENT

**For hospitalists and care transition teams** who struggle with incomplete, unverified discharge data that leads to preventable readmissions,

**Guardian One** is a clinical intelligence platform that creates continuously verified clinical narratives — catching medication discrepancies, unscheduled follow-ups, and unfilled prescriptions before they become readmissions.

**Unlike** EHR discharge modules (which freeze at discharge), ADT notification platforms (which react after events), and AI documentation tools (which generate text without verification),

**Guardian One** verifies every clinical claim against source systems, continues monitoring post-discharge, and keeps the patient in control of their data.

## 4. SALES STRATEGY

### Sales Motion: Enterprise, Clinical-Led

Health tech sales are won by clinical champions, not cold calls.

```
Clinical Champion Identified → Clinical Demo/Workshop → IT/Security Review →
Legal/Compliance (BAA) → Budget Approval (CFO) → Pilot Agreement →
Deployment → Outcomes Validation → Paid Contract → Expansion
```

**Average sales cycle:** 9–15 months (can be shortened to 6 months with strong champion)

### Sales Team Structure (Year 2)

| Role | Count | Focus |
|------|-------|-------|
| VP Sales | 1 | Strategy, key accounts, investor relationships |
| Enterprise AE | 2 | New hospital acquisition, quota-carrying |
| Clinical Solutions Engineer | 1 | Demos, clinical workflow mapping, pilot support |
| Customer Success Manager | 2 | Pilot management, expansion, renewals |

### Pricing Negotiation Anchors

**ROI framework for hospital CFOs:**

```
Average HRRP penalty:                    $220,000/year
Guardian One annual contract:            $300,000/year
Readmission reduction (conservative):    10–15%
Avoided readmissions × $15K avg cost:    $450,000–$675,000/year
Net ROI:                                 $150,000–$375,000/year (positive Year 1)
Payback period:                          <12 months
```

**Alternative framing:** "If Guardian One prevents just 20 readmissions per year, it pays for itself. Your hospital has 800+ readmissions annually."

### Investor Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Hospital sales cycles are too long" | "We target hospitals already spending on readmission reduction. Budget line items exist. Our clinical champion model shortens cycle to 6–9 months." |
| "Epic will just build this" | "Epic builds EHR features. Verification requires cross-system data (pharmacy, wearables, other EHRs). Epic won't build a patient-owned portable narrative that works outside Epic." |
| "How is this different from care coordination platforms?" | "CarePort and Bamboo Health move data between care settings. They don't verify it. We're a verification and error detection layer — complementary, not competitive." |
| "How do you get hospitals to share data?" | "We don't need hospitals to share. The patient authorizes access to their own data via SMART on FHIR. 21st Century Cures Act mandates this access." |
| "What about FDA regulation?" | "We're CDS exempt under 21st Century Cures Act Section 3060(a). We display and flag information for clinician review. We don't diagnose or recommend treatment." |
| "Can you prove readmission reduction?" | "That's exactly what the pilot is designed to measure. We're targeting a 10–15% relative reduction in CHF readmissions. Even 5% makes the ROI positive for the hospital." |

## 8. REGULATORY STRATEGY

### Compliance Roadmap

| Requirement | Timeline | Status |
|------------|----------|--------|
| HIPAA compliance (technical safeguards) | Pre-pilot | Architecture supports; needs formal assessment |
| Business Associate Agreements | Pre-pilot | Template ready; hospital-specific negotiation |
| SOC 2 Type I | Month 6 | Audit preparation in progress |
| SOC 2 Type II | Month 18 | Requires 6-month observation period |
| HITRUST CSF | Month 24 | Gold standard for health tech; enterprise requirement |
| State privacy laws (CMIA, SHIELD) | Ongoing | Architecture supports state-specific consent |
| FDA CDS exemption documentation | Pre-pilot | Maintain detailed records of intended use |
| 21st Century Cures Act compliance | Pre-pilot | Core to architecture; information blocking prevention |

### Regulatory Gaps to Monitor

1. **FDA AI/ML guidance evolution** — FDA is actively developing frameworks for AI in healthcare. Guardian One currently qualifies for CDS exemption, but regulatory landscape is shifting. Monitor closely.
2. **State-level AI regulation** — California, Colorado, and others are passing AI-specific laws. Ensure compliance with transparency and disclosure requirements.
3. **CMS readmission measure updates** — HRRP measure set may expand or change. Guardian One's clinical rules engine must adapt.
4. **FHIR version updates** — FHIR R5 is emerging. Plan for backward compatibility.
5. **Patient data portability rules** — ONC is expanding patient access requirements. Guardian One is well-positioned for this trend.

- Post-discharge events monitored per patient

- Sales cycle length (days from first contact to signed contract)
- Pilot-to-paid conversion rate

- 30-day readmission rate (pre/post Guardian One deployment)

- Patient satisfaction scores (care transition experience)
