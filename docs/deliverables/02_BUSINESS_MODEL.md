# Guardian One: Business Model Framework
## Venture-Backed, Rapid Scaling, Exit-Focused

---

## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

**Guardian One** is a patient-owned clinical intelligence platform that creates verified clinical narratives for hospital-to-community transitions. Multi-agent AI architecture orchestrates medication reconciliation, clinical error detection, wearable monitoring, and financial coordination — all under patient data sovereignty.

**Category:** Clinical AI / Care Transitions / Patient Safety
**Stage:** Pre-seed → Seed (working prototype, no revenue)
**Legal structure:** Delaware C-Corp (standard for VC-backed health tech)

## 3. REVENUE MODEL

### Primary: Enterprise SaaS (B2B2C)

| Tier | Target | Pricing | Includes |
|------|--------|---------|----------|
| **Pilot** | Single unit / department | $8K–15K/month | Up to 500 patients, single Epic instance, core Health Agent |
| **Standard** | Full hospital | $20K–30K/month | Unlimited patients, all agents, FHIR integration, support |
| **Enterprise** | Health system (multi-hospital) | $50K–100K+/month | Multi-site, cross-system narratives, custom rules, dedicated CSM |

**Pricing philosophy:** Per-facility flat fee (not per-bed, not per-patient). Hospitals hate variable pricing — they need budget predictability.

| Stream | Model | Timeline |

## 4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Direct Competitors

| Company | What They Do | Valuation/Funding | Guardian One Differentiation |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Bamboo Health** (fka Appriss/PatientPing) | Real-time ADT notifications, care collaboration | Acquired by Vista Equity ~$1.5B (2022) | Reactive notifications vs. proactive verified narratives |
| **CarePort (WellSky)** | Post-acute care referrals, discharge planning | Acquired by WellSky ~$1.35B (2021) | Referral logistics vs. clinical verification |
| **Collective Medical (PointClickCare)** | ED notifications, care collaboration | Acquired by PointClickCare (2021) | ED-focused vs. full transition narrative |
| **Pieces Technologies** | AI clinical documentation, discharge summaries | Series B, ~$100M+ valuation | AI-generated summaries vs. verified narratives from source data |
| **Abridge** | AI ambient clinical documentation | **$5.3B valuation** (2025, $300M Series E led by a16z) | Documentation capture vs. cross-system verification |
| **Hippocratic AI** | Healthcare AI agents | $1.64B valuation (2025, $141M Series B) | General-purpose health AI vs. care transition verification |
| **Ambience Healthcare** | Clinical AI workflow | $1.04B valuation (2025, $243M Series C) | Ambient documentation vs. cross-system narrative verification |

### Guardian One's Moat

1. **Verification layer** — Competitors generate or transmit documents. Guardian One *verifies claims against source systems*. This is a fundamentally different product.
2. **Patient data sovereignty** — Patient owns their narrative. This unlocks patient-authorized cross-system data sharing that EHR-locked competitors can't do.
3. **Multi-agent architecture** — Extensible to financial, scheduling, and social determinants. Not just clinical.
4. **Post-discharge loop** — Most competitors stop at discharge. Guardian One continues monitoring through pharmacy fills, wearables, and follow-up scheduling.

## 8. RISK MATRIX

| Risk | Severity | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Epic integration friction** | High | Medium | FHIR R4 is mandated; start with App Orchard listing; hire Epic-experienced engineers |
| **Long hospital sales cycles** | High | High | 12–18 month cycles expected; seed funding must cover 2 years of runway |
| **Regulatory reclassification** | Medium | Low | FDA CDS exemption clear for our use case (1,250+ AI devices authorized by July 2025); maintain documentation compliance |
| **HIPAA breach** | Critical | Low | Encrypt everything, SOC 2 / HITRUST, cyber insurance, minimal data retention |
| **Competitor feature parity** | Medium | Medium | Verification + sovereignty are architectural advantages, not features that can be bolted on |
| **Clinical validation timeline** | Medium | Medium | Start outcomes study at pilot launch; 6–12 months for publishable data |
| **Reimbursement uncertainty** | Medium | Medium | Don't depend on new CPT codes; sell on penalty avoidance and cost reduction |
